harte echtzeit (HE) is a scientist and sonic artist who works with algortihms, scientific data and live coding.

HE's interest is mainly in the interplay of abstract sounds and concrete, rhythmic structures. his music is driven by scientific data which seems to have no history or imagination on a human scale but rather on larger more complex scales in space and time. capturing the vast journey, time and energy which encompasses events that, for example emit gravitational waves, while also finding ways to communicate about it in a human scale language, plays a central role in HE's work. as a result, it leads to compositions which explore and experiment with sounds and soundscapes but also let rhythmic or melodic structures sneek in from time to time.

he is currently based in Naarm/Melbourne, Australia and one of the founding artists of Live Coders Naarm collective. he is als one co-founder of futur AAA festival and organizes algoraves, workshops and other events himself.

=>sonic experiments

gravitational waves

it took about 100 years for Einsteins prediction to become true, but in 2015 gravitational waves were detected for the first time by the Advanced LIGO instrument. these waves coming from the depths of space, carry energy and information about the history of the universe and hit us on a daily basis. with my project Gravitational Wave Field Recordings (GWFR), I use audio samples of detected gravitational waves and transform them into musique concréte-inspired pieces of sonic art. by utilizing techniques of live coding, I perform music with computer code which conveys the history of gravitational wave detection and the deep space. the music helps us to travel through space and time with rhythmic patterns, mysterious sound textures and experimental structures. the tracks explore space and time in a minimal yet sometimes dreamy as well as danceble way.

a set of tracks was released as the album ji kū kan on September 14th, 2024 via Call It Aything Records. it is available digitally and as a limited edition cassette tape. a press release for this album can be download here.

some tracks of the album were featured in radio shows and discussed in blogs [1, 2, 3, 4 ]. especially, AUDIO CRACKLE praises it for its uniqueness

'ji kū kan' is an atmospheric album of cosmic soundscapes - filled with demented "dance" rhythms, erratic pulsations, minimal techno, mesmerizing drones and experimental musique concrète. I can't claim to understand the technicalities of live coding, or how these compositions were created… but I can definitely appreciate the uniqueness of it, and enjoy this abstract aural adventure! An unnerving yet trance-inducing listening experience.

i have written a short text about my work in LIGO magazine #26 (pp. 20-21) titled Going abstract: what does the Universe sound like? which you can read here.
